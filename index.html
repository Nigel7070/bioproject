C1
Atlas Vertebra

The atlas (C1) is the first cervical vertebra, forming the joint connecting the skull and spine. It supports the weight of the head and allows nodding movements (yes motion).

Key Features:

  • No vertebral body: Unique ring-shaped structure
  • Anterior arch: Front portion with facet for dens
  • Posterior arch: Back portion with groove for vertebral artery
  • Lateral masses: Bear weight of the head
  • Transverse processes: Contain transverse foramen
  • Superior articular facets: Articulate with occipital condyles

Clinical Significance:

Atlas fractures (Jefferson fractures) can occur from vertical compression. The atlas lacks a spinous process and has unique anatomy crucial for head movement and stability.
