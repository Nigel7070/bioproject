C
Cervical (C1-C7)

7 vertebrae in the neck region. Smallest and most mobile vertebrae. Allow head rotation and flexion. C1 (Atlas) supports the skull, C2 (Axis) allows rotation.

T
Thoracic (T1-T12)

12 vertebrae in the chest region. Articulate with ribs forming the thoracic cage. Medium-sized vertebrae with limited mobility to protect vital organs.

L
Lumbar (L1-L5)

5 vertebrae in the lower back. Largest and strongest vertebrae, bearing most of the body's weight. Allow flexion and extension of the trunk.

S
Sacrum (5 fused)

5 fused vertebrae forming a triangular bone. Connects the spine to the pelvis. Transmits body weight to the hip bones.

Co
Coccyx (3-5 fused)

3-5 fused vertebrae forming the tailbone. Vestigial structure providing attachment for ligaments and muscles of the pelvic floor.
