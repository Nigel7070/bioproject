C
Cervical (C1-C7)

Seven vertebrae in the neck region. Smallest and most mobile vertebrae allowing head rotation and flexion. C1 (Atlas) supports the skull, while C2 (Axis) enables rotational movement.

T
Thoracic (T1-T12)

Twelve vertebrae in the chest region that articulate with ribs, forming the thoracic cage. Medium-sized with limited mobility to provide stability and protect vital organs.

L
Lumbar (L1-L5)

Five vertebrae in the lower back. Largest and strongest vertebrae, bearing most of the body's weight. Allow significant flexion and extension of the trunk.

S
Sacrum (5 fused)

Five fused vertebrae forming a triangular bone. Connects the spine to the pelvis through the sacroiliac joints and transmits body weight to the hip bones.

Co
Coccyx (3-5 fused)

Three to five fused vertebrae forming the tailbone. Vestigial structure providing attachment points for ligaments and muscles of the pelvic floor.
